Cox Proportional Hazards — Survival Analysis (ASCII)
======================================================

A classifier predicts: "will this employee leave? (yes/no)"
A survival model predicts: "how long until this employee leaves?"

Survival curve S(t) = P(still employed at time t)

   1.0 |\
       | \
       |  \
   S(t)|   \____
       |        \____
       |             \____
       |                  \____
       |                       \___
   0.0 +--------------------------------> time (months)
       0    6    12   18   24   30

The Cox model fits per-feature multipliers on a baseline hazard:

   h(t | x) = h_0(t) * exp(beta_1*x_1 + beta_2*x_2 + ...)

  - h_0(t)          baseline hazard (depends on time, not features)
  - exp(beta^T x)   multiplier      (depends on features, not time)

Key assumption: PROPORTIONAL HAZARDS
  the multiplier is constant over time
  if feature x doubles your hazard at month 1,
  it also doubles it at month 24

Validate with lifelines.statistics.proportional_hazard_test
(if it fails, split the model by tenure band)

Features used in AttriSense (NOT including Tenure_Months,
which IS the time variable):
  - Base_Salary
  - Dept_Code
  - Manager_Tenure_Months

Output:
  per-employee survival curve stored in `survival_curves` table,
  surfaced as the survival sparkline on the SHAP Insights tab.
