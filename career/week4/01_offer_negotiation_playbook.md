# Offer Negotiation Playbook

> Read this **twice** before any phone call where the recruiter says
> the words "we'd like to make you an offer." Do not improvise comp.

---

## Before any number is mentioned

1. **Don't disclose your current / desired number.** When asked,
   answer: *"I want to make sure the role is a strong mutual fit
   first. Once we're both confident on that, I'm happy to talk
   numbers — and I'm sure we can land somewhere fair to both sides."*
2. **Get them to share the band first.** Most US states (CA, NY, WA,
   CO) require pay-band disclosure on job posts. Read the post
   carefully before the call.
3. **Map your BATNA.** Best Alternative To a Negotiated Agreement.
   For most people coming out of MSBA, BATNA is one of:
   - Another active offer (strongest leverage)
   - An ongoing process at a comparable company
   - Continuing the public AttriSense work (open-source maintainer
     positioning is real leverage in a tight market)

---

## When the offer arrives

| Component | Default ask | Strong ask | Walk-away |
|-----------|-------------|------------|-----------|
| **Base** | +5% on their first | +10–15% with a comp comparison | Below your floor |
| **Sign-on bonus** | $5k–10k | One-month-of-base | None offered |
| **Equity** | Match / +10% on RSUs | One additional level of grant | Pre-IPO with no liquidity path AND below-market base |
| **Title** | Mid-level | One title up if exp warrants | Title that caps growth in year 1 |
| **Start date** | 3–4 weeks out | 6 weeks out | Demand to start in <2 weeks |
| **Visa support** | Confirmed in writing | Multi-year support letter | Verbal-only commitments |
| **Remote / hybrid** | Stated explicitly in offer letter | Locked for 12 months | "We'll figure it out later" |

## The negotiation script (3-step)

**Step 1 — Express genuine excitement:**
> *"Thank you so much — I'm genuinely excited about the role and the
> team. The work on [specific thing] is exactly what I want to be
> spending the next phase of my career on."*

**Step 2 — State the gap, not the floor:**
> *"There's one piece I'd love to talk through. Based on the market
> data I've gathered for [role title] in [location] — including
> three [active conversations / offers / Levels.fyi medians] —
> the band I've been seeing is **[X – Y].** I'm grateful for the
> offer, and I'd love to find a number we both feel is fair given
> the level of work I'd be bringing in."*

**Step 3 — Ask, then go quiet:**
> *"Is there flexibility on the base, or would it be easier on your
> side to bridge it through sign-on / equity / [other component]?"*
>
> Then **stop talking.** Whoever speaks first negotiates. Let them
> propose the bridge.

---

## When they say "this is our final offer"

It probably isn't, but treat the words as final.

> *"I appreciate that. Can I have until [end of week / Monday] to
> review with my partner / advisor and come back with a clear yes?"*

Then use the time. Re-read the offer letter line by line. Check
equity vesting cliff, refresh grants, severance, IP assignment
clauses (CRITICAL — see the patent guide), non-compete, and
relocation tax gross-up.

---

## Hard rules — never break

1. **Never** accept verbally, then re-negotiate. Get the offer
   *letter* first, then negotiate from the document.
2. **Never** say "my floor is X." Say "the band I've been seeing is
   X – Y." Bands give them a target; floors give them a ceiling.
3. **Never** negotiate against another offer you don't actually
   have. Recruiters check.
4. **Never** sign without reading the **IP assignment clause** —
   AttriSense is yours, MIT-licensed, and an over-broad clause can
   sweep it into the company on day one. If the clause is broad,
   add a written carve-out for AttriSense before signing.
5. **Always** get the final number, title, start date, equity grant
   size, and visa terms in writing in a single offer letter before
   you say yes.

---

## After accepting

- **Tell the people who helped.** Thank-you notes (`05_90_day_roadmap.md`
  has a list).
- **Withdraw from other processes** politely and in writing.
- **Update LinkedIn**, but do *not* change your title until **Day 1**
  of the new role — tempting fate.
- **Take 5 days off.** Whatever the offer letter says. Re-read this
  playbook in 18 months.
