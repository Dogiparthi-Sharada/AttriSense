# Cold-ish DM Templates — 5 variants

> **Rule:** all 5 templates assume you have *some* connection — a
> mutual, shared school, shared previous post. Pure cold DMs don't
> work. The 5 below are **warm-ish** outreach.
>
> Send 5 on Tuesday May 12 *after* the launch is live. Send 3 more
> on Wednesday May 13. **Total: 8 over two days. Stop there.**

---

## 1. Recruiter at a target company (you've never met)

**Subject (LinkedIn message — no subject; first line must hook):**

> Hi [First name] — I just launched **AttriSense**, a five-modality
> open-source workforce-intelligence platform (live at
> attrisense.streamlit.app) and noticed [Company] is hiring AI/ML
> Engineers. Would a 15-min walkthrough be useful, or should I send
> a tighter 60-second video first?
>
> No pressure either way — happy to just pass the GitHub link.

**Why this works:** asks them which format *they* prefer, which is
both respectful and lower-friction than "can we hop on a call?"

---

## 2. Hiring manager (warm intro from a mutual)

> Hi [First name] — [Mutual] suggested I reach out. I just launched
> **AttriSense** — five-modality retention platform with Cox PH
> survival, EconML T-Learner causal uplift, and a salted-hash
> Review_ID layer that blocks reviewer anchoring bias before it
> reaches the screen. Live demo: attrisense.streamlit.app, 7-page
> IEEE paper in the repo.
>
> Saw [Company] is hiring on the [team]. Would a 20-min conversation
> be useful, even if there's no immediate role?

**Why this works:** explicitly de-risks the conversation ("even if
there's no role") — managers reply twice as often when no answer is
required.

---

## 3. Author / engineer whose work you cite

> Hi [First name] — your [paper / blog post / talk] on [specific
> thing] shaped a design choice in **AttriSense** (a five-modality
> open-source workforce-intelligence platform I just launched).
> Specifically: [one sentence describing the choice — e.g. "your
> point about SMOTE-after-split was the reason I rebuilt my CV
> pipeline"]. Wanted to say thank you, and to share the demo in case
> the operational side is interesting: attrisense.streamlit.app.
>
> 7-page IEEE paper in the repo if you have a flight to fill.

**Why this works:** giving credit before asking for anything. Even
if they never reply, this is the right thing to do.

---

## 4. Alumni from your school working at a target company

> Hi [First name] — fellow CSUEB MSBA here. Just launched my capstone
> open-source: **AttriSense**, a five-modality workforce-intelligence
> engine (live demo: attrisense.streamlit.app). Saw you're at
> [Company] on [team] — would love your read on the dashboard for
> 10 minutes if you have it. No agenda beyond getting better
> feedback than my advisor can give me on the production side.

**Why this works:** explicit "no agenda," explicit "you know things
I don't" framing. Alumni respond.

---

## 5. Active LinkedIn engager (commented on someone else's relevant post)

> Hi [First name] — saw your comment on [post] — your point about
> [specific thing they said] is exactly the trade-off I hit building
> **AttriSense**. I just launched it (open-source, MIT, demo at
> attrisense.streamlit.app) and the [specific feature that maps to
> their comment] is the part that took longest to get right.
>
> Would value 15 minutes if you're up for it — or just a quick reply
> on whether you'd handle [specific design decision] differently.

**Why this works:** specificity proves you read what they wrote.
"Would handle X differently" is a softer ask than "can I pick your
brain."

---

## Universal rules for all 5

- **Reply rate target:** ≥ 25%. If you're below 15%, the post itself
  isn't doing the work — fix the post, not the DMs.
- **Never** send the DM and the LinkedIn comment on the same person
  in the same hour. Stagger.
- **Always** end with a low-friction next step ("a 60-second video"
  or "a quick reply on X"). Never "let me know what works."
- **No follow-up for 5 business days.** If still no reply, *one*
  soft follow-up: *"Wanted to make sure my last note didn't land in
  spam. No pressure either way."*
- **Stop after one follow-up.** Move on.
