# Recruiter 2nd-Touch Templates — soft follow-up (Wed May 20)

> **When:** 5 business days after the Tue May 12 launch DM, no reply.
> **Tone:** soft, link-light, low ask. Reference what's *new* since
> the first touch.

---

## Template A — for recruiters / TA who didn't reply

> Hi [First name] — wanted to make sure my last note didn't land in
> spam. Since I wrote, AttriSense picked up [X stars / Y demo
> sessions / a write-up in Z], and I just published an architecture
> carousel walking through the five modalities.
>
> No pressure either way — happy to pass the demo link
> (attrisense.streamlit.app) if a 60-second look is more useful than
> a call.

---

## Template B — for hiring managers / EMs who replied once and went quiet

> Hi [First name] — appreciated the [thing they said] last week.
> Quick update: I just shipped a vulnerability post on the three
> production lessons (SMOTE-after-split, the RAG provider fallback,
> the Review_ID identification-bias layer) — thought that one might
> be especially relevant given your point about [specific thing].
>
> Still happy to do a 20-min walkthrough if there's a window. If not,
> I'll keep my head down and ping again only when there's something
> meaningfully new.

---

## Template C — for the "let's circle back later" reply

> Hi [First name] — circling back per your suggestion. Two new things
> since we last spoke: an 8-slide architecture carousel (Tue May 19)
> and a 7-page IEEE paper submitted to Big Data 2026 (Industry Track)
> with the full per-department fairness audit.
>
> If now is a better time for the conversation we sketched, I'm
> wide open Wed/Thu/Fri this week. If not, I'll plan to follow up
> after the IEEE acceptance lands.

---

## Universal rules

- **Mention exactly ONE new thing** — carousel, paper, or
  vulnerability post. Not all three. Listing too many feels desperate.
- **Always offer a low-friction alternative** to a call: "60-second
  video," "demo link," "one-paragraph reply on X."
- **Stop after two unanswered follow-ups.** Move them to the
  Closed log in `../week2/05_pipeline_tracker.md` with a note. Don't
  burn a third touch on someone who hasn't replied to two.
- **Never** reply-all, never CC mutuals, never ask the original
  introducer to nudge. If they wanted to chase, they would.

---

## What to do if no one is replying

If by Friday May 22 you have **zero** "Replied" rows from your second-
touch batch, the problem isn't the second touch — it's the *first
DM*. Pull up `../week2/03_cold_dm_templates.md` and audit:

- Are you opening with a *specific* connection? (Mutual, school,
  shared post.) Pure cold doesn't work.
- Is your low-friction alternative **truly low-friction**? "60-sec
  video" beats "20-min call." "One-line reply" beats "let me know
  what works."
- Are you sending too many at once? 5–8 over two days is right.
  20+ in a day looks like spam, even with a perfect template.
